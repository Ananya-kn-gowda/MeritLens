The Problem

Traditional systems ignore context.

A 3.2 GPA from a student in a low-income household with no tutors, attending an underfunded rural school — is not the same as a 3.2 from a student with every advantage. Yet most systems treat them identically.

Raw Scores Lie

Raw academic scores reflect access to resources as much as raw ability. Students with expensive tutors and prep courses have a structural advantage.

Underfunded Schools

Government and rural schools often lack labs, libraries, and experienced teachers — making achievement significantly harder to attain.

Income Barriers

Students from low-income families juggle part-time work, caregiving duties, and financial stress — factors invisible to any standard scoring rubric.

Hidden Talent Lost

Countless brilliant minds are filtered out early — not because they lack potential, but because the system never had a lens wide enough to see them.

Our Solution

Introducing the Grit Multiplier.

How we calculate adjusted merit:

Adjusted Score = Raw Score × Grit Multiplier

Grit Multiplier factors:

  • Family income bracket (lower = higher multiplier)
  • School type & funding level
  • Regional opportunity index
  • Access to academic resources
  • Socioeconomic stress indicators
01

Contextual Scoring

We don't lower standards — we raise context. Every score is evaluated against the environment it was achieved in, giving each student a fair fighting chance.

02

Transparent Algorithm

The Grit Multiplier is not a black box. Every factor is documented, auditable, and calibrated against real socioeconomic data.

03

Equitable Outcomes

By adjusting for context, institutions discover candidates they would have otherwise overlooked — leading to more diverse, resilient cohorts.
